Project Overview

This project involved designing a Power Distribution Board (PDB) to manage and protect power delivery for multiple communication modules in a Nucleo U575-based system. The board integrated protection circuitry to safeguard against overcurrent and reverse polarity, as well as biasing circuits to ensure stable voltage levels for sensitive components. The schematic and PCB layout were created in Altium Designer, with careful trace routing to maintain signal integrity and meet current-handling requirements. Testing was performed with the Nucleo U575 to validate proper power sequencing, fault protection response, and stable operation under varied load conditions.

Basic Functionality

  • Distributes regulated power from the main supply to multiple communication modules in a Nucleo U575-based system.
  • Integrates protection circuitry to prevent damage from overcurrent and reverse polarity events.
  • Uses biasing circuits to maintain stable voltage levels for sensitive components.
  • Monitors and maintains proper power sequencing to ensure safe module startup and shutdown.
  • Provides reliable operation under varied load conditions while preserving signal integrity.

This project was completed as part of the UBC Sailbot Project Polaris initiative in collaboration with the Electrical Communications subteam. I was primarily responsible for designing the Power Distribution Board (PDB) schematic and PCB layout in Altium Designer, integrating the protection and biasing circuits to ensure stable and safe power delivery.

Compact 2-layer PCB design for a Nucleo U575-based power distribution system, integrating protection circuitry, biasing circuits, and optimized trace routing for stable, reliable power delivery to multiple communication modules.
